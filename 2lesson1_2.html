  1. Color and Contrast - Using colors to your advantage can help with the emphasis and overall tone of your website.
  2. Lay-out - Making sure the lay-out looks clean and easy to follow can make it so your website can be easily accessible.
  3. Alignment - The alignment of certain elements can bring harmony to your website, make sure to take advantage of this!
  4. White Space - White space can be used to create certain images with the use of shadows, sometimes to define a shape you can use a form that is shapeless.
  5. Audience - Your target audience should always be your front and foremost priority. If the website is for professionalism use minimalism as much as possible or if its for children then make sure its engaging enough for them to understand.

User Experience (UI) Design is the process of creating products or services that provide meaningful experiences for users, involving many different areas of product development including branding, usability, function, and design.

  1. Simplicity - Make it simple! For the sake of both you and your user, as sometimes the more complicated a website can be the more of a load it'll be for the website and hard for the user to follow the entire point.
  2. Consistency - Similar with the palette always make sure your elements are consistent and can create its own harmony along with the different types of elements.
  3. Reading Patterns - Observe the way a website and how they use certain attention seeking elements that ensure that their user will be able to follow the right track when navigating the website. Each website is different and use different kinds of techniques (SEE ALIGNMENT ABOVE)
  4. All Platform Design - Making sure your website can be accustomed to many types of devices makes it so many others can also access it and otherwise boost the experience for the user.
  5. Don't use your powers for evil - Please always be mindful with the things you create, take responsibility for something that's under your name.
